# SlipWatch

**Authors:** Love Solberg, Tobias Sandström, Emma Edlund

---

## Problem statement

Falls are a leading cause of injury-related deaths among elderly people. As AI finds its way into all fields of technology, its only a question of when it will be applied to monitoring elderly people. This problem requires not only a solution for elderly peoples safety, but also for this technology to be explainable and understandable so it could be improved upon, with a future vision of having no fall-related deaths occur simply because no one was there to help.

## Solution approach

Our solution approach to this problem is to use an automated AI-detection system that uses cameras to detect if a person has fallen, which would alert a social worker or relative about the situation and prevent the fallen person to be left on the floor. Compared to other solutions such as having an alert button bracelet, keeping a phone or some other wearable solution, our solution provides the person with freedom and safety by not having to keep track of any device. If the person suffers from a disease such as dementia, or has gotten a concussion from the fall, this system would still be able to alarm about the situation.

## Real-world application

This program is primarily intended to be set up in elderly peoples homes, especially in high risk areas such as staircases, kitchens and bathrooms. This technology also has the potential to be specialized for other types of clients such as those with MS, Parkinsons disease, or other neurological conditions that increase fall risk.
